Search tables and metrics, explore field values, and construct or run queries against your data.

You can use Metabase to:

**Answer ad-hoc business questions in natural language:**  
"What was our revenue by region last month, compared to the month before?"

**Investigate anomalies and trends:**  
"Refunds are up this month. Show me the top contributors by product category."

**Ground engineering work in real production data:**  
"What columns does the orders table have, and what does a sample row look like? I'm writing a migration."
